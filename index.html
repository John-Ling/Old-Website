About Me

Who?


A bit about me


Hi! My name is John Ling.

I am a 17 year old student living in Malaysia currently taking A levels. I take mathematics, physics and computer science as most of them align with my own interests. While I have a fascination with many fields of technology however I've found my interest is mainly in programming. Personal projects are my favourite aspect of programming as I enjoy building practical applications to help myself or others. From conceptualisation and design to the small triumphs debugging code, I enjoy all of it.

Outside of programming many of my hobbies mostly originate from skills I had learnt for projects. These include electronics, 3D printing and playing piano.

I am also the founder of a charity named Plugged In Malaysia which refurbishes broken computers and distributes them to two charities. As of now, 166 computers and counting have been donated. I continue to manage the charity to this day.

What?


My tools for the job


I have been programming for 2 years in a variety of different languages such as Python, C# and Javascript. Over the course of my journey, I've picked up many more skills needed to build intuitive and scalable applications. All of my skills are self-taught through online resources or MOOCs such as CS50.

When picking new technologies to learn, I typically don't discriminate based on their use in industry. I'm equally as likely to learn legacy Winforms over modern Angular; the same rule applies in reverse. My mindset when learning new technology is to focus on the principles they utilise rather than unique details.

My reasoning for this approach stems from second-hand experience watching content creators online and accounts from friends working in the field. From large corporations to up and coming startups, the tools and technologies they use vary differently. A developer that can adapt to quickly use new tools will be more valuable than a developer who cannot.

With how often tech can change, such as the current prevelance of blockchain and a decentralised "Web 3" or the trend to build desktop applications with web-based languages and Electron, a modular approach to learning allows me to quickly adapt to new progressions and changes to the standard paradigm.
