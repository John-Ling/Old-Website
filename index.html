What?

The tools I use

I have been programming for 3 years in a variety of different languages such as Python, C# and Javascript. All of my skills are self-taught through online resources or MOOCs such as CS50.

When picking new technologies to learn, I don't always go towards the cutting-edge, "trendy" frameworks. I'm not really obsessed with a particular "Tech Stack". A website built using, PHP, vanilla Javascript and Bootstrap is just as good as one built using React, Typescript and Tailwind CSS as long as the end result accomplishes what the programmer wanted.

I think the KISS (Keep It Simple Stupid) philosophy is a very valid approach to development so I like to use the right tool for the job rather then the hottest one.

Why?

Why code?

As corporate as it sounds, I enjoy applying theory and skills I know to build things for myself and others to assist in their everyday lives. Whether it be a Raspberry Pi powered dashboard, facial detection tracking systems, productivity apps or a compiler so future A level students can learn pseudocode easier, in the end all my projects are made with a desire to assist others even in a minor way. Just like how electronics and mechanical systems can be used to solve a problem, software is another method with its own benefits and drawbacks.

Many tools are available online for free making programming accessible to all backgrounds. The same applies for knowledge. It's only in this day and age where one can watch undergrad lectures for compilers before they even go to university.

The accessibility of free professional tools and easy access to knowledge makes software engineering one of the easiest fields to get into in my opinion. Itt's that ease of access combined with nearly limitless potential that makes it fun.
