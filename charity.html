Charity

Plugged In Malaysia

In June of 2021, after my IGCSEs, I founded a charity named Plugged In Malaysia at 15 after learning of the "Digital Divide" within education-the split between those who have access to online learning and those who do not. Many cases, students will be hardware-limited with a slow or unusable computer that cannot run the software for education.

I founded PIM to take in those slow or unsuable computers, repair, refurbish and distribute them. I personally work with two Malaysian charities: JREC and MyReaders. JREC runs 5 UNHCR schools across Malaysia, providing computer access to over 850 students. MyReaders is a volunteer led literacy programme to teach underprivileged children how to read.

As of writing, 166 computers have been donated.

PIM has garnered a variety of supporters from companies such as AmBank, Chubb, and DHL to members of the wider community including neighbors and teachers. All have given their support and pledged computers.
