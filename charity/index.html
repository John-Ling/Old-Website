Charity

Plugged In Malaysia

In June of 2021, after my IGCSEs, I founded a charity named Plugged In Malaysia at 15 years old. My main motives for starting the charity was partly a desire to help others in a way I personally felt was meaningful and an active interest in repairing and breathing new life into old laptops.

PIM's operation is incredibly simple. People donate their old, broken computers and I fix them up into a usable machine. I then send the computers to one of two charities MyReaders and JREC. MyReaders provide remedial literacy programs for illiterate children while JREC manages 6 schools across Malaysia housing 900 students.

Malaysia has a prominent "Digital Divide". There is a large split between those with and without access to technology. COVID-19 has only made this problem worse by locking out less fortunate families out of online education because of their lack of computer access. Trying to reduce that divide by just a little bit by allowing lower-income families to access online learning is what PIM hopes to do.

As of writing, 166 computers have been donated. The donors range from neighbors to teachers to companies such as AmBank, Chubb and DHL.
